Value Proposition

According to a study conducted by the Cleveland Clinic in 2022, 41%, 51%, 51%, and 58% of Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X, and Baby Boomers, respectively, are actively trying to eat healthy. The primary percieved barriers were:

  • Too expensive (said 46% of respondents)
  • Too little time to cook/prepare (said 46% of respondents)
  • Lack of Knowledge (said 20% of respondents)

Personally, I have encountered these barriers, which make eating healthy food challenging. My work-around has been to only buy healthy food at home to create a healthy default and to do my best when I eat out. The goal is to create a viral food product that people can afford, that I and my future-teammates are proud to serve, and customers are proud to buy. For example, I remember asking the franchisee of a Chic-fil-A back where I went to high-school about ingredients they used to cook their food, and he got very defensive and wanted to talk about other things. I would like to be part of an organziation that is more than happy to disclose the ingriedients of the product they sell. When I worked for Canes in high school, I was responsible for 'marinading' the chicken with literally just MSG.
