Values and Ethics: The human heart longs for a battle to fight, an adventure to live, and a beauty to rescue (John Eldredge, Wild at Heart). I try to follow the following advice a Godly person once gave me: “Achieve the maximal amount you can achieve. Be the servant who, when his master left, earned the most return on that which he had been entrusted. Do what you do to God’s glory. ... If Godly ambition has placed us in a position of power, then we should empty ourselves of personal ambition/selfishness and take on the form of a servant/slave to become the leader whom God wishes us to be” (Mr. B on Matthew 10:15 & 10:42-45). With this perspective, I can “eat and drink and find enjoyment in all the toil with which one toils under the sun the few days of his life that God has given him.... For he will not much remember the days of his life because God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart” (Ecclesiastes 5:18-20). Thus, as Augustine says, “Love God and do what you will.”

Breaking Rules, Skepticism, and Libertarian Tilt: General Douglas MacArthur famously said that you are remembered for the rules you break. I strive to honor Christ, submitting to Biblical authority. In practice, this looks like obeying a boss at work (because I have chosen to work there and submit myself to their authority), carrying out their instructions in service of Christ - my ultimate authority - “not as eye-service, as people-pleasers, but as bondservants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart, rendering service with a good will as to the Lord and not to man, knowing that whatever good anyone does, this he will receive back from the Lord, whether he is a bondservant or free” (Ephesians 6:6-8). Those with rational Asperger's (Thiel), an eye that “cannot choose but to see” (Wordsworth), and the “poet, the artist, the sleuth--whoever sharpens our perception tends to be antisocial, rarely ‘well-adjusted,’ he cannot go along with currents and trends” (McLuhan, Quentin Fiore, 88). To summarize the previous statement, diversity of thought, character, and friends are infinitely valuable. “Treat humanity... never merely as a means to an end, but always at the same time as an end” (Kant, Metaphysics on Morals). My college admissions essay centered around my decision to avoid trying “to be” (John Boyd), and to focus on “doing” (John Boyd) - I still hold to that.

Goals and Aspirations: I love building (facing hard problems and making a big impact). How you do one thing is how you do everything. If something isn't worth all of me, it isn't worth doing at all, or I need to be giving my all anyways. I plan to use my mathematical background to study the world around me, software as a tool, and psychology to understand how to implement solutions in a humane, human-in-the-loop, and people-first way.
